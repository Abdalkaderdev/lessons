# Business Documents with AI

## What You'll Learn

- How to create professional contracts, proposals, and Statements of Work (SOWs)
- How to write Memorandums of Understanding (MOUs)
- How to format invoices correctly
- The basics of legal language — what to include and what to watch for
- How to turn AI-generated documents into reusable templates

> Professional documents protect you, set clear expectations, and make your business look credible. This lesson shows you how to create them without hiring a lawyer for every one.

---

## Why Business Documents Matter

Imagine this: you complete a 3-month project for a client. They pay only half and claim you didn't deliver what was agreed. You have no written contract.

Or: you bring on a partner for a new business. Six months later, you disagree on who owns the intellectual property. There's no MOU.

Professional documents aren't bureaucracy — they're protection. They also signal to clients and partners that you operate like a serious business, not a freelancer who handles things informally.

With AI, creating these documents takes 15 minutes, not 3 days.

---

## Important Disclaimer

> AI-generated legal documents are a strong starting point, but they are not a substitute for legal advice. For high-value contracts, partnership agreements, or anything that could result in significant legal liability, have a qualified lawyer review the document before signing.

That said, for most small business needs — basic client contracts, service proposals, invoices, and informal MOUs — AI does an excellent job.

## Part 1: Client Contracts

### What a Basic Service Contract Includes

1. **Parties** — Full names and details of everyone signing
2. **Scope of Work** — Exactly what you will and won't deliver
3. **Timeline** — Start date, milestones, end date
4. **Payment Terms** — Amount, schedule, method, late fee policy
5. **Revisions Policy** — How many revisions are included
6. **Intellectual Property** — Who owns the work product
7. **Confidentiality** — What information stays private
8. **Termination Clause** — How either party can end the contract
9. **Dispute Resolution** — How disagreements are handled
10. **Signatures** — Date and sign

### Creating a Contract with AI

> Prompt: "Create a professional service contract for a freelance [graphic designer / social media manager / web developer / consultant — insert your role]. The contract should be between me ([Your Name], service provider) and a client. Include: scope of work section (to be filled in), payment terms with a 50% deposit upfront and 50% on completion, 2 rounds of revisions included, IP ownership transferred to client upon final payment, a confidentiality clause, and a 14-day notice termination clause. Use clear, professional language."

After generating:

> Prompt: "Review this contract. Are there any common clauses I'm missing that would protect me as the service provider? Add them."

> Prompt: "Simplify the legal language in Section 4 (Payment Terms) so a non-lawyer can easily understand it."

### Making It Your Template

Once you have a contract you're happy with, save it as a template. Replace client-specific details with placeholders like `[CLIENT NAME]`, `[PROJECT DESCRIPTION]`, `[TOTAL FEE]`.

> Prompt: "Convert this contract into a reusable template by replacing all client-specific details with placeholder labels in brackets. Preserve the full structure and formatting."

- [ ] Add at least one clause that addresses something specific to your industry (e.g., for photographers: usage rights; for developers: hosting and maintenance terms).
- [ ] Convert it to a template with placeholders.

## Part 2: Project Proposals

### What Is a Proposal?

A proposal is a document you send to a potential client before they hire you. It outlines:
- The problem you're solving
- Your proposed approach
- What you'll deliver
- Your timeline
- Your price

A well-written proposal wins business. A weak one loses it — even if you're more qualified than the competition.

### Structure of a Winning Proposal

1. **Cover Page** — Your name/company, client name, date, project title
2. **Executive Summary** — 2–3 sentences summarizing the problem and your solution
3. **Understanding of the Problem** — Show you've listened and understood their needs
4. **Proposed Solution** — Your approach, methodology, and why it works
5. **Scope of Work** — Specific deliverables, broken down clearly
6. **Timeline** — Phase-by-phase schedule
7. **Investment** — Pricing, broken down by phase or deliverable
8. **Why Us** — Brief section on your qualifications and relevant experience
9. **Next Steps** — Clear call to action (e.g., "Please sign and return page 6 to begin")

### Creating a Proposal with AI

> Prompt: "Write a professional project proposal for the following scenario: I am a [your role] and a client has asked me to [describe the project]. The client's main goal is [goal]. I plan to deliver [list deliverables] over [timeline] for a total of [price]. Structure it with: executive summary, problem statement, proposed solution, scope of work, timeline, investment, and next steps. Keep the tone professional and persuasive."

### Tailoring the Tone

> Prompt: "Rewrite the 'Why Us' section to feel more confident and less salesy. I want it to sound like a trusted expert, not someone trying to convince them."

### Exercise 2
- [ ] Write a full proposal for a hypothetical project (use a real scenario from your life if possible — a freelance job you want, a client you're approaching).
- [ ] Review the pricing section: does it clearly justify the investment?
- [ ] Have AI critique your proposal: "What weaknesses might a potential client see in this proposal? How would you address them?"

## Part 3: Statement of Work (SOW)

### SOW vs. Contract vs. Proposal

| Document | Purpose | When Used |
|----------|---------|-----------|
| Proposal | Win the business | Before the client says yes |
| Contract | Legal agreement | When both parties commit |
| SOW | Detailed work scope | Often attached to or part of a contract |

A Statement of Work is more detailed than a proposal's scope section. It defines exactly what will be done, when, by whom, and to what standard.

### Creating an SOW with AI

> Prompt: "Create a Statement of Work for the following project: [describe the project in detail — what you're doing, for whom, what the deliverables are, what the timeline is]. The SOW should include: project objectives, scope of work (broken into phases), deliverables with acceptance criteria, timeline with milestones, roles and responsibilities, and out-of-scope items. Format it professionally."

The "out-of-scope" section is critical — it explicitly states what you are NOT responsible for, which prevents scope creep.

> Prompt: "Based on this project description, what are 5 things that would commonly be out of scope? Add an Out-of-Scope section to the SOW."

### Exercise 3
- [ ] Create an SOW for your current or hypothetical project.
- [ ] Make sure there is a clear "Out of Scope" section with at least 3 items.
- [ ] Check: does every deliverable have a clear acceptance criterion (i.e., how will you and the client know it's done)?

## Part 4: Memorandum of Understanding (MOU)

### What Is an MOU?

An MOU is a document between two parties that describes an intended working relationship or collaboration — without being a full legal contract. It's commonly used for:
- Business partnerships before formalizing the legal structure
- Joint ventures or collaborations
- Agreements with suppliers, referral partners, or co-founders

An MOU is less binding than a contract, but it documents that both parties understood and agreed to the terms at a point in time.

### Creating an MOU with AI

> Prompt: "Draft a Memorandum of Understanding between myself ([Name, Role]) and [Partner Name, Role]. We are agreeing to [describe the collaboration — e.g., refer clients to each other, co-develop a product, share resources]. Include sections on: purpose of the MOU, roles and responsibilities of each party, revenue sharing arrangement (if applicable), duration, confidentiality, and how either party can exit the arrangement. Keep it professional but not overly legalistic."

### Exercise 4
- [ ] Think of a real or hypothetical collaboration (a referral partner, a co-founder, a supplier arrangement).
- [ ] Draft an MOU for that collaboration using the prompt above.
- [ ] Identify: what is the single biggest risk to each party in this arrangement, and is it addressed in the MOU?

## Part 5: Professional Invoices

### What a Professional Invoice Must Include

1. The word "Invoice" and a unique invoice number
2. Your name/business name and contact details
3. Client's name and contact details
4. Invoice date and payment due date
5. Itemized list of services with quantities and unit prices
6. Subtotal, taxes (if applicable), and total amount due
7. Payment instructions (bank details, payment link, accepted methods)
8. Late payment policy (e.g., "Invoices unpaid after 14 days incur a 2% monthly fee")

### Creating an Invoice Template with AI

> Prompt: "Create a professional invoice template for a freelance [your service type] business. Include all standard invoice fields. Use a clean, professional layout described in plain text. Add a payment terms section at the bottom with a 14-day payment period and a 2% monthly late fee clause."

For a recurring client, you want a template you can fill in quickly:

> Prompt: "Convert this invoice into a fillable template with placeholder text in brackets for all variable fields (client name, dates, services, amounts). Keep the formatting consistent."

### Exercise 5
- [ ] Create your personal invoice template using the prompt above.
- [ ] Fill it in as if invoicing a real or hypothetical client for work you've done.
- [ ] Check: does it look like something you'd be proud to send?

## Part 6: Professional Formatting Tips

AI can generate the content — but presentation matters too. Here are formatting principles for all business documents:

### Do This
- Use a consistent, professional font (Arial, Calibri, or Georgia — not Comic Sans)
- Include your logo or business name as a header on every page
- Number your pages
- Use section headers to make navigation easy
- Use tables for pricing, scope, or timeline information
- Include a signature block at the end of contracts and MOUs
- Save documents as PDF before sending (PDF can't be accidentally edited)

### Ask AI to Help with Formatting

> Prompt: "Reformat this document so it has clear section headers, uses a professional structure, and would look appropriate in a business setting. Describe the layout recommendations so I can apply them in Word or Google Docs."
