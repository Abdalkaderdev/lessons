# Market Research with AI

## What You'll Learn

- How to use AI to research competitors quickly and effectively
- How to estimate market size without being a data analyst
- How to identify trends before they go mainstream
- How to build a SWOT analysis with AI assistance
- How to define and find your target audience

> This lesson is hands-on. By the end, you will have real market research done on a business idea of your choice.

---

## Why Market Research Matters

Most businesses fail not because the founder wasn't smart or hardworking — they fail because no one wanted what they were selling.

Market research answers three critical questions:
1. **Is there a real problem to solve?**
2. **Who has this problem?**
3. **Is anyone else already solving it — and how well?**

In the past, this kind of research took weeks and cost thousands of dollars. With AI, you can get 80% of the way there in an afternoon.

## Part 1: Competitor Analysis

### What Is Competitor Analysis?

Competitor analysis means understanding who else is operating in your space — what they offer, how they price it, what customers say about them, and where they fall short.

### How to Do It with AI

You don't need to spend hours on Google. Start with a well-structured prompt.

> Prompt: "I'm starting a [type of business] targeting [audience] in [location/market]. Who are the main competitors in this space? For each one, summarize: what they offer, their pricing model, their strengths, and common customer complaints."

**Example:** Let's say you want to start a meal-prep delivery service for college students in Dubai.

> Prompt: "I want to start a meal-prep delivery service targeting university students in Dubai. Who are the main competitors in this market? For each competitor, tell me: what they offer, their pricing, their strengths, and what customers complain about."

AI will return a structured breakdown. Your job is to:
- Note the gaps (what are they NOT doing well?)
- Look for price points (what is the market used to paying?)
- Spot differentiation opportunities (what could make you different?)

- [ ] Run the competitor analysis prompt above using your idea.

## Part 2: Market Sizing

### Why It Matters

"Everyone will want this" is not a market. You need to know approximately how many people could buy your product and how much money is in the space.

### The TAM / SAM / SOM Framework

| Term | Meaning | Example |
|------|---------|---------|
| TAM | Total Addressable Market — everyone who could ever buy | All university students in the UAE |
| SAM | Serviceable Addressable Market — who you can realistically reach | Students in Dubai with smartphones |
| SOM | Serviceable Obtainable Market — your realistic first-year target | 500 students in your first semester |

### How to Use AI for Market Sizing

> Prompt: "Help me estimate the market size for a [product/service] targeting [audience] in [location]. Break it down using the TAM, SAM, SOM framework. Use any publicly available data you know about and note where I should verify numbers."

**Important:** AI can give you frameworks and estimates, but always double-check key numbers using Google, government statistics, or industry reports. Use AI to structure your thinking, not as your only source of truth.

## Part 3: Trend Identification

### Why Trends Matter

Timing matters in business. A business that rides a rising trend grows faster and cheaper than one fighting against the current.

### How to Spot Trends with AI

> Prompt: "What are the current and emerging trends in the [industry] industry over the next 2–3 years? Focus on [region/market]. What behaviors, technologies, or cultural shifts are driving these trends?"

After getting AI's response, validate by checking:
- **Google Trends** (free) — search your category and see if interest is growing
- **TikTok / Instagram** — what content is going viral in your space?
- **Reddit communities** — what are people asking for that doesn't exist yet?

- [ ] Go to Google Trends and check the search volume for your main keyword over the past 5 years.
- [ ] Note: is your market growing, stable, or declining?

## Part 4: Finding Your Target Audience

### The Mistake Most Beginners Make

Saying "my target audience is everyone aged 18–35" is not a target audience. It's a wishlist. The more specific you are, the more effective your marketing, your product design, and your messaging will be.

### Building a Customer Profile with AI

> Prompt: "Help me build a detailed customer profile (persona) for my ideal customer. My business is [describe it]. Who is this person? Include: age range, income level, daily routine, pain points related to my product, where they spend time online, what they value, and what would make them choose my product over a competitor."

This gives you what marketers call a **customer persona** — a detailed description of your ideal buyer.

## Part 5: SWOT Analysis with AI

### What Is a SWOT Analysis?

A SWOT analysis is a simple but powerful tool used in every serious business plan:

| | Internal | External |
|-|----------|----------|
| Positive | **Strengths** | **Opportunities** |
| Negative | **Weaknesses** | **Threats** |

- **Strengths** — What do you have that competitors don't? (skills, location, network, pricing)
- **Weaknesses** — What are your current limitations? (no funding, no brand, no experience)
- **Opportunities** — What market conditions favor you? (growing trend, underserved audience)
- **Threats** — What could hurt your business? (new competitor, regulation, economic downturn)

### How to Build One with AI

> Prompt: "I'm starting a [business type] targeting [audience] in [location]. Based on what you know about this market, help me build a SWOT analysis. Be honest about the weaknesses and threats — I want a realistic picture."

Then refine it:

> Prompt: "Based on this SWOT, what is the single biggest risk I should address before launching? And what is the strongest opportunity I should focus on first?"
