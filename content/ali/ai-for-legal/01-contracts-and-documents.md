# Understanding Contracts with AI

## What You'll Learn

- How to use AI to read and understand legal documents in plain English
- What red flags to look for in NDAs, employment contracts, and lease agreements
- How to ask AI to explain specific legal terms and clauses
- How to create basic contract templates for common business situations
- When to stop using AI and call a real lawyer

> IMPORTANT LEGAL DISCLAIMER: AI tools (including Claude, ChatGPT, and all others mentioned in this lesson) are not lawyers and cannot provide legal advice. Everything generated by AI about legal topics is informational only. Before signing any contract, making any legal decision, or taking action based on legal questions, always consult a qualified lawyer licensed in your jurisdiction. This lesson teaches you to be a more informed reader of legal documents — not to replace professional legal counsel.

---

## Why Understanding Contracts Matters for Every Business Owner

At some point in your business life, you will sign:
- An NDA before sharing your business idea with a potential partner
- An employment contract when you hire your first employee
- A lease for your office or retail space
- A supplier agreement, a client services agreement, a terms of service
- A loan agreement or investment term sheet

Most people sign these documents without reading them properly — because they're long, dense, and written in a language designed to be hard to understand. This is dangerous. A contract you don't understand is a commitment you can't evaluate.

AI changes this. You can now paste a contract into Claude or ChatGPT and get a plain-English summary in under two minutes.

## Part 1: Using AI to Understand Legal Documents

### The Basic Approach

Copy the text of any legal document and ask AI to explain it. Start broad, then zoom in on the parts that confuse you.

> Prompt: "I'm going to paste a legal document. Please: (1) summarize what this document is and what I'm agreeing to in plain English, (2) highlight any clauses that are unusual, risky, or heavily favor the other party, (3) list any key obligations I would be taking on, and (4) flag any deadlines or time-sensitive terms. Here is the document: [paste document text]"

Then follow up on specific clauses:

> Prompt: "Clause 7.3 says [paste the clause]. What does this mean in plain English? Is this standard or unusual? What would happen to me if I violated this?"

### NDAs (Non-Disclosure Agreements)

An NDA is an agreement to keep certain information confidential. You'll encounter these:
- Before sharing your business idea with a potential partner, investor, or vendor
- When hiring employees who will have access to sensitive information
- When entering into partnerships or negotiations

**What to look for in an NDA:**

| Clause | What to Check |
|--------|---------------|
| Definition of "Confidential Information" | Is it too broad? Does it cover things you need to talk about freely? |
| Duration | How long does the confidentiality last? 1 year? Forever? |
| Permitted disclosures | Can you share with lawyers, accountants, employees who need to know? |
| Mutual vs. one-sided | Is only one party bound to confidentiality, or both? |
| Jurisdiction | Which country's laws govern this agreement? |
| Remedies | What happens if there's a breach? Damages? Injunctions? |

> Prompt: "I've been asked to sign an NDA before a meeting with a potential business partner. Here is the NDA: [paste text]. Is this a standard NDA or does it contain any clauses that are unusual or that I should negotiate? What are the 3 most important things I should know before signing this?"

### Employment Contracts

When you hire your first employee — or when you're offered a job — the employment contract governs the entire working relationship.

**Key sections to understand:**

- **Compensation and benefits** — salary, bonuses, equity, leave entitlement
- **Probationary period** — what can happen during probation vs. after confirmation
- **Working hours and location** — are these fixed or flexible?
- **Intellectual property (IP) clause** — who owns work created during employment?
- **Non-compete clause** — can the employee work for a competitor after leaving?
- **Termination terms** — what notice is required? What triggers immediate termination?
- **Confidentiality** — what can the employee not share, and for how long?

> Prompt: "I'm reviewing an employment contract for someone I want to hire. Please analyze this employment contract and tell me: (1) the key obligations on both sides, (2) the termination terms, (3) whether the IP ownership clause is standard or gives the company unusually broad rights, (4) whether the non-compete is enforceable or overly broad, and (5) anything that might cause a problem with UAE labor law. Here is the contract: [paste text]"

> Important: Employment law varies significantly by country. In the UAE, for example, MOHRE (Ministry of Human Resources & Emiratisation) governs most employment relationships and has mandatory minimum standards. Always verify that your employment contracts comply with local law.

### Lease Agreements

A commercial or residential lease is often one of the largest financial commitments a small business makes.

**Red flags to watch for:**

- **Automatic renewal clauses** — the lease renews automatically unless you give 60–90 days notice
- **Rent escalation clauses** — annual rent increases of 5–15% built into the contract
- **Personal guarantee** — you personally guarantee rent even if the business folds
- **Assignment and subletting restrictions** — can you bring in a co-tenant or sell the lease?
- **Fit-out and restoration obligations** — must you restore the space to original condition?
- **Force majeure** — what happens if you can't use the space (as happened with COVID)?

> Prompt: "I'm reviewing a commercial lease agreement for a retail space. Please analyze this lease and: (1) summarize the key financial obligations including base rent, service charges, and any escalation clauses, (2) flag any unusual or risky clauses, (3) identify any clauses that heavily favor the landlord, (4) explain the termination and break-clause options, and (5) tell me what I would be responsible for at the end of the lease. Here is the lease: [paste text]"

## Part 2: Red Flags to Look For

### Universal Red Flags in Any Contract

Regardless of the type of contract, watch for these patterns:

**Overly broad definitions** — If "confidential information" means "anything you see, hear, or think about in our presence," that's too wide.

**Unlimited liability** — If you can be held liable for any amount without a cap, negotiate a liability cap.

**One-sided termination rights** — If only the other party can terminate immediately, but you must give 6 months' notice, that's unbalanced.

**Governing law in another country** — If you're in Dubai and a contract is governed by California law, any dispute becomes extremely expensive.

**Waiver of rights you didn't mean to waive** — Some contracts include language where you agree not to bring class action suits, not to seek certain remedies, or not to sue in certain jurisdictions. Read these carefully.

**Automatic escalation clauses** — Rent, subscription, or service fees that increase automatically without your approval.

**Ambiguous scope of work** — In service contracts, "other duties as required" language can be used to justify almost anything.

### Getting AI to Hunt for Red Flags

> Prompt: "Review this contract as if you were a lawyer protecting my interests as the [buyer/tenant/employee/service provider]. Identify every clause that (1) could be used against me, (2) limits my rights unusually, (3) is vague enough to be interpreted many ways, or (4) would be expensive or difficult for me to comply with. Be thorough — I'd rather know about a risk before signing than discover it later."

## Part 3: Getting AI to Explain Legal Terms

### A Glossary of Common Legal Terms

| Term | Plain English Meaning |
|------|-----------------------|
| Indemnify | Agree to compensate the other party if something goes wrong |
| Severability | If one clause is invalid, the rest of the contract still stands |
| Force majeure | Events outside anyone's control (floods, wars, pandemics) that excuse non-performance |
| Jurisdiction | Which country or state's courts will handle any disputes |
| Arbitration | Disputes are settled privately, not in court |
| Liquidated damages | A pre-agreed penalty amount for specific breaches |
| Waiver | Giving up a right you would otherwise have |
| Representations and warranties | Statements made as facts that the other party relies on |
| Assignment | Transferring your rights or obligations to a third party |

### The Best Way to Ask AI to Explain Legal Language

> Prompt: "This contract clause says: '[paste clause]'. Please explain: (1) what this means in plain English, (2) what I'm agreeing to or giving up, (3) whether this is standard language or unusual, and (4) what would happen practically if this clause were triggered."

For unfamiliar terms:

> Prompt: "What does '[legal term]' mean in the context of a [type of contract] in [your country]? Give me a plain English explanation and one real-world example of when this would matter."

## Part 4: Creating Basic Contract Templates

### When You Can Use AI-Generated Templates

AI-generated contract templates are appropriate for:
- Early-stage, low-risk business relationships between trusted parties
- Internal documents (onboarding agreements, internal policies)
- First drafts that will be reviewed and modified by a lawyer
- Low-value service agreements

They are NOT appropriate (without professional review) for:
- Significant financial transactions
- Employment contracts in regulated industries
- IP licensing and ownership
- Investment agreements
- Anything cross-border or cross-jurisdiction

### Types of Templates You Can Create

**Freelance / Service Agreement**

> Prompt: "Create a simple freelance service agreement template for a [type of service] provider working with clients in [country]. Include: parties' details, scope of services, payment terms, intellectual property ownership (client owns all deliverables), confidentiality, revisions policy, termination clause, and governing law. Keep it under 2 pages. Plain English where possible."

**Non-Disclosure Agreement (NDA)**

> Prompt: "Create a mutual NDA template for two businesses exploring a potential partnership in [country/region]. Both parties will share confidential information. Include: definition of confidential information, exclusions (public information, already known), obligations of each party, duration (2 years), what happens on termination, and remedies for breach. One page maximum."

**Letter of Intent (LOI)**

> Prompt: "Draft a non-binding letter of intent template for a business partnership discussion. Include: the purpose of the proposed partnership, preliminary terms being discussed, a non-binding disclaimer, exclusivity period (if applicable), confidentiality, and timeline for completing a formal agreement."

## When to Stop Using AI and Call a Lawyer

Use AI to get informed. Use a lawyer to get protected. The line is:

**Use AI when:**
- You want to understand what you're reading before a meeting
- You want to generate a first draft to discuss with a lawyer
- You want to know what questions to ask
- The transaction is low-stakes and between parties you trust

**Call a lawyer when:**
- Any amount over AED 50,000 (or your local equivalent) is at stake
- You're signing employment contracts in a regulated industry
- You're entering a partnership or shareholder agreement
- You're licensing intellectual property
- You're in dispute or potential litigation
- You don't understand the contract after AI explains it

A one-hour consultation with a commercial lawyer costs AED 500–1,500 in Dubai. That's cheap compared to what a contract dispute costs.
