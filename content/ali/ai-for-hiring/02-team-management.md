# Team Management with AI

## What You'll Learn

- How to run performance reviews that actually improve performance
- How to structure 1-on-1 meetings so they're useful, not awkward
- How to give and collect team feedback with AI-generated frameworks
- How to build an onboarding checklist that gets new hires productive fast
- How to create training materials and internal documentation with AI

> Managing people is a skill. Most people are never taught it — they're just promoted and expected to figure it out. This lesson gives you a head start.

---

## Why Team Management Is a Business Skill, Not Just an HR Function

When you manage people well:
- Projects get done on time and to a higher standard
- People stay longer (reducing costly turnover)
- Problems surface early, before they become crises
- Your team gets better over time because feedback actually lands

When you manage people poorly:
- Top performers leave (they always have options)
- Problems hide until they explode
- You spend more time fixing mistakes than growing the business

AI doesn't replace good management — it removes the friction that prevents managers from doing the basics consistently.

## Part 1: Performance Reviews with AI

### What Makes a Performance Review Effective

A good performance review is not a surprise. It covers:
- **Achievements** — what did this person actually accomplish this period?
- **Areas for growth** — what specific behaviors or skills need development?
- **Goals for next period** — clear, measurable commitments going forward
- **Support needed** — what does the manager or company need to provide?

The worst reviews are vague ("you did well, keep it up") or one-sided (manager lectures, employee nods).

### Building a Performance Review Template

> Prompt: "Create a structured performance review template for a [job title] role at a [type of company]. Include sections for: self-assessment (3–4 questions), manager assessment (covering performance against goals, skills demonstrated, areas for growth), a rating system from 1–5 with definitions for each level, SMART goals for the next period, and a section for both parties to sign off. Keep it practical — it should take no more than 45 minutes to complete."

Use [Claude](https://claude.ai) for this — ask it to produce a version for both the manager and the employee.

### Writing a Specific Review with AI

Once you have review notes from a conversation or observation, AI can help you write them clearly and professionally.

> Prompt: "I need to write a performance review for a [job title] I manage. Here are my rough notes about their performance this quarter: [paste notes]. Help me turn this into a professional, specific, and constructive performance review. Highlight their achievements clearly, frame areas for improvement in a growth-focused way, and suggest 2 specific SMART goals for next quarter."

### Handling Difficult Reviews

> Prompt: "I need to write a performance review for an employee who has been underperforming. Specifically: [describe the performance issues]. Help me write a clear, honest, and professional review that acknowledges positives while being direct about the issues. Include an improvement plan with specific expectations and a timeline. The tone should be firm but supportive — the goal is to help them improve, not to build a case for termination."

> Important: For serious underperformance situations or potential terminations, always involve a qualified HR professional or employment lawyer to ensure you are following local labor law. In many countries (including the UAE), there are specific legal requirements around performance improvement plans and terminations.

## Part 2: 1-on-1 Meeting Agendas

### Why 1-on-1s Are the Most Important Meeting You Run

A weekly or bi-weekly 1-on-1 with each direct report is where:
- You hear about problems before they blow up
- Your team member feels seen and supported
- You give targeted feedback and coaching
- Trust is built over time

Most managers skip them when things get busy. This is exactly when they matter most.

### The Structure of an Effective 1-on-1

A 30-minute 1-on-1 might look like:
- **5 min** — Check-in: how are they doing personally/professionally?
- **10 min** — Their agenda: what's on their mind? What do they need?
- **10 min** — Your agenda: updates, feedback, priorities
- **5 min** — Wrap-up: action items, decisions made

The key rule: **their agenda comes first.** This is not your update meeting — it's their support meeting.

### Generating 1-on-1 Agendas with AI

> Prompt: "Create a 1-on-1 meeting agenda template for a [weekly/bi-weekly] 30-minute check-in between a manager and a [job title]. Include: a brief personal check-in, open questions for the employee to share updates and blockers, a section for manager feedback, a priorities review, and an action items log. Make it a shared document where both people can add items before the meeting."

For specific situations:

> Prompt: "I have a 1-on-1 meeting tomorrow with a team member who I know is feeling frustrated about [situation]. Help me create an agenda that opens space to discuss this honestly without putting them on the defensive. Include 3 specific questions I could ask to understand their perspective."

> Prompt: "Generate 10 strong 1-on-1 questions for a manager to ask a [job title] to understand their motivation, career goals, and any hidden frustrations. Avoid generic questions — make them specific and thought-provoking."

## Part 3: Team Feedback Frameworks

### Types of Feedback in a Team Setting

**Upward feedback** — team members share feedback about their manager.
**Peer feedback** — team members share feedback about each other.
**Downward feedback** — manager shares feedback with team members.
**360-degree feedback** — all three combined.

Each requires a different approach. Upward and peer feedback need psychological safety — people must believe there are no negative consequences for honesty.

### Building a Feedback Framework with AI

> Prompt: "Create a lightweight 360-degree feedback framework for a small team of [number] people. Include: a set of 8–10 questions for peer feedback, 6–8 questions for upward feedback (employee rating their manager), and guidance on how to collect, anonymize, and share results in a way that feels safe and constructive. The team is at a [startup/growing business/established company]."

### Writing Feedback Yourself with AI Assistance

When you have feedback to give but aren't sure how to phrase it:

> Prompt: "I need to give constructive feedback to a team member. The situation is: [describe it]. The behavior I observed was: [describe]. The impact was: [describe]. Help me write this feedback clearly using the SBI framework (Situation-Behavior-Impact), in a tone that is direct but not aggressive. I want to focus on the behavior, not the person."

### Collecting Anonymous Feedback

Use [Google Forms](https://forms.google.com) (free) for anonymous team feedback surveys. AI can write the questions; Google Forms handles collection and anonymization.

> Prompt: "Write a 10-question anonymous team health survey for a small business team. Cover: workload and stress levels, clarity of goals and priorities, quality of communication from leadership, sense of recognition and value, collaboration quality, and one open-ended question. Keep questions specific and answerable on a 1–5 scale where possible."

## Part 4: Onboarding Checklists

### Why Onboarding Is Often Terrible — and Why It Matters

Most small businesses "onboard" new hires by saying "here's your laptop, ask if you need anything." The result: the new hire is unproductive for months and leaves within a year.

Structured onboarding:
- Gets people productive 2–3x faster
- Reduces anxiety and early-stage mistakes
- Shows new hires that the company is organized and cares
- Sets clear expectations from day one

### The 30-60-90 Day Onboarding Framework

| Period | Focus |
|--------|-------|
| Week 1 | Tools access, team introductions, company overview, culture |
| Days 8–30 | Role fundamentals, shadowing, first small wins |
| Days 31–60 | Increasing independence, first real deliverables |
| Days 61–90 | Full productivity, owning their role, first performance check-in |

### Creating an Onboarding Checklist with AI

> Prompt: "Create a comprehensive onboarding checklist for a new [job title] joining a [type of company] of [size] people. Break it into: Day 1 checklist (logistics, access, introductions), Week 1 checklist (culture, tools, processes), 30-day milestones, 60-day milestones, and 90-day milestones. For each milestone, include the goal, who is responsible (HR, manager, or the new hire), and what success looks like."

For remote teams:

> Prompt: "Adapt the onboarding checklist for a fully remote new hire joining an async-first team. What changes about the tools setup, introductions, and culture immersion when someone never comes into an office?"

## Part 5: Training Material Creation

### What Training Materials Are

Training materials help your team learn the skills and knowledge they need to do their jobs well. They include:
- **Standard Operating Procedures (SOPs)** — step-by-step instructions for routine tasks
- **Training guides** — deeper explanations of how and why things are done
- **Video scripts** — used to record internal training videos
- **Quizzes** — to test understanding after training

### Creating SOPs with AI

> Prompt: "Write a standard operating procedure (SOP) for [specific task or process]. The process involves [brief description of steps]. The person following this SOP is a [job title] who [level of experience]. Format it with: Purpose, Scope, Tools Required, Step-by-Step Instructions (numbered), Common Mistakes to Avoid, and a checklist at the end."

**Example:**

> Prompt: "Write a standard operating procedure for handling a customer complaint over WhatsApp at a small retail business. The customer service rep has 0–6 months of experience. Include: Purpose, Tools (WhatsApp Business, our CRM), step-by-step process from receiving the complaint to resolution, escalation criteria (when to involve a manager), and a checklist the rep can tick through for each complaint."

### Writing Training Scripts with AI

> Prompt: "Write a 5-minute video training script for new [job title] hires on [specific topic]. The tone should be friendly and clear — not corporate. Include: an opening hook that explains why this matters, the core content broken into 3 key points, a practical example, and a summary. Assume the viewer has just started and needs this explained from scratch."

### Building a Quiz with AI

> Prompt: "Create a 10-question knowledge check quiz for team members who have just completed training on [topic]. Include a mix of multiple choice (7 questions) and short answer (3 questions). Provide the correct answers in a separate section. The quiz should test practical understanding, not just memorization."

## Putting It All Together

Here is what a well-managed team looks like when you use these tools consistently:

- **Weekly** — 1-on-1 with each direct report (30 min each)
- **Monthly** — Team feedback check (pulse survey, 5 questions)
- **Quarterly** — Formal performance reviews using your template
- **Annually** — 360-degree review cycle
- **Ongoing** — SOPs updated as processes change, new hire onboarding follows the 30-60-90 plan

This takes a few hours to set up. It saves hundreds of hours — and prevents the loss of people you can't afford to lose.
