# Content Strategy with AI

## What You'll Learn

- How to build a content calendar that keeps you consistent without burning out
- How to write blog posts with AI that actually rank on Google
- How to write video scripts for YouTube, Instagram Reels, and TikTok
- How to repurpose one piece of content across multiple platforms
- How to use analytics to understand what's working and do more of it

> Content is how brands are built in 2025. Every business — no matter how small — needs a consistent content presence. AI removes the hardest part: the blank page. This lesson gives you a complete system.

---

## Why Content Strategy Matters

Most businesses post content inconsistently. They put up three Instagram posts in a week, then go silent for three weeks. They write one blog post, never write another. This inconsistency kills results — algorithms punish it and audiences forget you.

A content strategy means:
- Knowing what to create (topics, formats, keywords)
- Knowing where to publish (platforms)
- Knowing when to publish (schedule)
- Knowing how to measure success (analytics)

AI makes this manageable for one person.

## Part 1: Building a Content Calendar

### What Is a Content Calendar?

A content calendar is a plan that shows what content you'll publish, when, and where. It removes the daily decision of "what should I post today?" and helps you maintain consistency.

### Choosing Your Platforms

Don't try to be everywhere at once. Pick 2 platforms maximum when starting:

| Platform | Best For | Content Type |
|----------|----------|--------------|
| Instagram | Visual brands, B2C, lifestyle | Reels, Stories, Carousels |
| TikTok | Young audiences, entertainment | Short videos |
| LinkedIn | B2B, professional services | Text posts, articles |
| YouTube | Education, long-form content | Videos (7–20 min) |
| Blog / Website | SEO, long-form authority | Articles (600–2,000 words) |
| Email | Owned audience, conversions | Newsletters, campaigns |

### Creating a Content Calendar with AI

> Prompt: "Create a 30-day content calendar for a [type of business] that posts on [platforms]. My audience is [describe]. My business goals for this month are [e.g., launch a new product, grow Instagram by 500 followers, drive traffic to my website]. Include: content topics for each post, the content format (reel, carousel, blog post, etc.), the goal of each piece (awareness, engagement, conversion), and a suggested posting schedule. Aim for [X posts per week] across all platforms."

**Example:**

> Prompt: "Create a 30-day content calendar for a personal styling and fashion consulting business in Dubai. Platforms: Instagram and LinkedIn. My audience on Instagram is women 25–40 interested in professional styling; on LinkedIn it's HR managers and professionals. Goal: grow Instagram by 400 followers this month. Post 4x/week on Instagram (mix of Reels and carousels) and 2x/week on LinkedIn (text posts and occasional articles). Include: topic, format, and goal for each post."

### Using Notion AI for Your Calendar

[Notion](https://notion.so) has a built-in AI assistant and is excellent for managing a content calendar:
1. Create a new database in Notion
2. Add columns: Title, Platform, Format, Status, Publish Date, Link
3. Use Notion AI to generate content ideas directly inside your database

> Prompt (inside Notion AI): "Suggest 10 content ideas for a [type of business] targeting [audience] for [platform]. For each, give a title and one-line description."

## Part 2: Blog Posts with AI

### Why Blog Posts Are Still Worth Writing

A well-written blog post targeting the right keyword can drive free traffic from Google for years. Social media posts disappear from feeds within hours. Blog posts compound.

A good blog post strategy paired with SEO (covered in Lesson 1 of this module) is one of the highest-ROI investments a business can make.

### The Structure of a High-Performing Blog Post

1. **Title** — includes the target keyword, is specific, answers "why should I read this?"
2. **Introduction** — hooks the reader, states the problem, previews the solution
3. **Body** — broken into sections with H2/H3 headings, answers the search intent thoroughly
4. **Practical examples** — makes abstract advice concrete
5. **Conclusion** — summarizes key points, tells reader what to do next
6. **CTA** — email signup, product link, related article

### Writing a Blog Post with AI

> Prompt: "Write a 800-word blog post targeting the keyword '[keyword]'. Structure: engaging introduction (1 paragraph), 4–5 body sections with H2 headings, a practical example or case study, and a conclusion with a call to action. My business is [describe]. The reader is [describe their pain point or goal]. Tone: [professional/conversational/expert]. Include the keyword naturally in the title, introduction, 2–3 subheadings, and throughout the body."

### Improving an AI-Generated Blog Post

Raw AI output often needs editing. The content is usually accurate but can feel generic. Add:
- **Your personal stories or client examples** — makes it real
- **Specific numbers and results** — "our client went from 2% to 8% conversion rate"
- **Your opinions** — what do you actually think about this topic?
- **Local context** — if your audience is in Dubai, make UAE-specific references

> Prompt: "Here is a blog post I've drafted: [paste post]. Make these improvements: (1) make the introduction more engaging with a hook — a surprising stat or provocative question, (2) add 2 more specific examples or use cases, (3) strengthen the conclusion with a clearer next step, (4) improve the overall flow between sections. Keep my voice and any personal details I've included."

## Part 3: Video Script Writing

### Why Video Is Non-Negotiable in 2025

Video is the dominant content format. Instagram Reels, TikTok, and YouTube Shorts collectively reach billions of daily viewers. Text and images still matter — but video builds trust and audiences faster.

You don't need expensive equipment. A smartphone, decent lighting, and a well-written script are enough to create content that works.

### Types of Video Content and Their Scripts

**Short-form video (15–60 seconds) — Instagram Reels, TikTok, YouTube Shorts**

> Prompt: "Write a 45-second video script for Instagram Reels about [topic]. My audience is [describe]. Structure: a hook in the first 2 seconds (make it specific and surprising), the core value/teaching in the middle (3–4 punchy points), and a CTA at the end (follow, save, comment, or visit link in bio). The tone should feel like I'm talking to a friend, not presenting to a boardroom."

**Medium-form video (3–8 minutes) — YouTube, LinkedIn**

> Prompt: "Write a YouTube video script about [topic]. Length: approximately 5 minutes when spoken at a normal pace. Structure: hook (30 seconds), context and why this matters (1 min), main content broken into 3 key points (2.5 min), a real example or case study (45 seconds), conclusion and CTA (15 seconds). Include direction notes in brackets (e.g., [cut to screen recording] or [show product]). My business: [describe]. Audience: [describe]."

**Explainer / Product Video (60–90 seconds)**

> Prompt: "Write a 90-second explainer video script for [product or service name]. Include: an opening problem statement that resonates with the viewer, how [product/service] solves it, 3 key benefits (one sentence each), social proof element, and a closing CTA. Write it to be spoken in a clear, confident, conversational voice."

### Using AI to Generate Script Hooks

The hook (opening seconds) determines whether someone keeps watching or scrolls away.

> Prompt: "Write 10 hook options for a video about [topic]. For each, give one sentence that would make someone stop scrolling. Include: 2 curiosity hooks, 2 problem/pain point hooks, 2 bold statement hooks, 2 question hooks, and 2 story hooks."

### Tools for Video Scripts

- [Claude](https://claude.ai) — excellent for long, structured scripts
- [ChatGPT](https://chat.openai.com) — good for short-form and hook generation
- [Gamma](https://gamma.app) — AI presentation builder that can also visualize scripts and slide-based video content

## Part 4: Repurposing Content Across Platforms

### The One-to-Many Content System

Creating content from scratch for every platform is exhausting and unsustainable. Smart content creators build a "core" piece of content and repurpose it into multiple formats for different platforms.

**The repurposing map:**

```
One long blog post (2,000 words)
├── 5 short LinkedIn posts (one insight each)
├── 1 Instagram carousel (the key points as slides)
├── 3 Instagram/TikTok Reels (one idea per video)
├── 1 email newsletter (adapted for your list)
└── 10 Twitter/X threads (one per section)
```

One week of content — from a single piece of work.

### Repurposing with AI

> Prompt: "I have a blog post / video / podcast episode about [topic]. Here is the content: [paste or summarize it]. Repurpose this into: (1) a 250-word LinkedIn post with a strong hook, (2) a 5-slide Instagram carousel outline (slide 1 = hook, slides 2–4 = key points, slide 5 = CTA), (3) a 45-second Instagram Reels script, and (4) a 200-word email newsletter version. Keep the core message consistent but adapt the tone and format for each platform."

> Prompt: "Turn this blog post into 5 separate LinkedIn text posts: [paste blog post]. Each post should stand alone as a useful insight. For each one: write a hook (first line only), the body (100–150 words), and end with a question to drive comments."

### Content Repurposing Workflow

1. Write the long-form piece (blog post or YouTube script) — this is your core
2. Publish it on your website or YouTube
3. Extract 5 key insights using AI
4. Turn each insight into a short social post
5. Combine the key insights into a newsletter
6. Create a Reels or TikTok from the most interesting angle

## Part 5: Analytics Basics — Understanding What Works

### Why Analytics Matter

Creating content without looking at analytics is like driving without looking at the speedometer. You might be going the right direction — but you don't know.

Analytics tell you:
- What content resonates with your audience
- Which platforms are driving real results (traffic, followers, revenue)
- What time your audience is most active
- Whether your CTAs are working

### Key Metrics by Platform

**Instagram/TikTok:**
- Reach — how many unique people saw your content
- Engagement rate — (likes + comments + saves + shares) ÷ reach × 100
- Follower growth — net new followers per week/month
- Profile visits and link clicks — are people taking action?

**Blog/Website:**
- Sessions and pageviews — how much traffic are you getting?
- Bounce rate — are people leaving immediately?
- Average time on page — are they actually reading?
- Organic traffic — how many visitors came from Google?

**Email:**
- Open rate, CTR, unsubscribe rate (covered in Lesson 2)

### Using AI to Interpret Your Analytics

> Prompt: "Here are my content analytics for the past 30 days: [paste data or describe]. Instagram: [engagement rates, top posts, growth]. Blog: [page views, top articles]. Email: [open rates, click rates]. Help me interpret what's working and what isn't. Based on this data, what should I do more of, less of, and differently in the next 30 days?"

### Setting Up Basic Analytics

- **Google Analytics 4** ([analytics.google.com](https://analytics.google.com)) — free, tracks everything on your website
- **Instagram Insights** — built into your Instagram app (switch to Creator or Business account)
- **Mailchimp Analytics** — built into every campaign

> Prompt: "I'm setting up Google Analytics for the first time on my [type of website]. What are the 5 most important things I should track and why? For each metric, tell me: what it measures, what a good vs. bad number looks like for my type of business, and what action I should take if the number is low."

## Putting It All Together: A Sustainable Content System

Here's a weekly routine that keeps you consistent without burning out:

**Monday (2 hours):**
- Review last week's analytics — note what performed best
- Generate this week's content ideas based on your calendar
- Outline the week's content

**Tuesday–Wednesday (2 hours total):**
- Write the core piece (blog post or YouTube script)
- Repurpose into social posts and email

**Thursday (1 hour):**
- Schedule all posts for the week
- Send or schedule the email newsletter

**Total: ~5 hours/week** for a full content presence across blog, social, and email.
