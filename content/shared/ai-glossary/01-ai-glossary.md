# AI Glossary

> A quick-reference guide to the AI terms you will encounter across these lessons. Definitions are kept short and practical -- come back here whenever you hit an unfamiliar word.

---

## A

**AI (Artificial Intelligence)** -- Software that can perform tasks that normally require human thinking, such as understanding language, recognizing images, or making decisions.

**Algorithm** -- A step-by-step set of instructions a computer follows to solve a problem or complete a task. Think of it like a recipe: follow the steps, get the result.

**API (Application Programming Interface)** -- A way for two programs to talk to each other. When an app uses ChatGPT behind the scenes, it connects through an API.

**Automation** -- Using technology to perform tasks without human help. For example, a script that automatically sorts your email is automation.

---

## B

**Bias** -- When an AI system consistently favors or disfavors certain groups, ideas, or outcomes because of patterns in its training data. A biased model might, for example, associate certain jobs with only one gender.

**Bot** -- A program that performs automated tasks. A chatbot is a bot that can hold a conversation with you.

---

## C

**Chatbot** -- An AI program you can have a text conversation with, like Claude or ChatGPT.

**ChatGPT** -- A popular AI chatbot made by OpenAI. It uses GPT models to generate text responses.

**Claude** -- An AI assistant made by Anthropic, designed to be helpful, harmless, and honest. Claude powers the conversations in many of these lessons.

**Context Window** -- The maximum amount of text an AI model can "see" at once, including your messages and its replies. A bigger context window means the AI can remember more of your conversation.

**Coding Assistant** -- An AI tool that helps you write, debug, or explain code. Examples include GitHub Copilot and Claude.

---

## D

**Data** -- Information in any form -- numbers, text, images, audio -- that computers can process. AI models learn from huge amounts of data.

**Deep Learning** -- A branch of machine learning that uses neural networks with many layers. It powers things like image recognition and language understanding.

---

## E

**Embedding** -- A way of turning words, sentences, or other data into lists of numbers so that a computer can measure how similar they are. Words with similar meanings end up with similar numbers.

---

## F

**Fine-tuning** -- Taking an AI model that has already been trained on general data and training it a bit more on a specific topic so it becomes an expert in that area.

---

## G

**Generative AI** -- AI that creates new content -- text, images, music, code, or video -- rather than just analyzing existing content. ChatGPT and DALL-E are generative AI tools.

**GPT (Generative Pre-trained Transformer)** -- A family of large language models created by OpenAI. "Pre-trained" means the model learned from a massive amount of text before you ever used it.

**Grounding** -- Connecting an AI's responses to real, verified information (like a database or search results) so it is less likely to make things up.

---

## H

**Hallucination** -- When an AI confidently generates information that is incorrect or completely made up. Always double-check facts from AI, especially names, dates, and statistics.

---

## I

**Inference** -- The moment an AI model actually runs and produces an output, such as answering your question. Training teaches the model; inference is when it uses what it learned.

---

## L

**LLM (Large Language Model)** -- An AI model trained on enormous amounts of text so it can understand and generate human language. Claude, GPT-4, and Gemini are all LLMs.

---

## M

**Machine Learning (ML)** -- A type of AI where the computer learns patterns from data instead of being told exact rules. The more data it sees, the better it gets.

**Model** -- The trained "brain" of an AI system. A model contains all the patterns and knowledge the AI learned during training.

**Multimodal** -- An AI that can work with more than one type of input or output, such as text, images, and audio together. GPT-4o and Gemini are multimodal models.

---

## N

**Natural Language Processing (NLP)** -- The branch of AI focused on helping computers understand, interpret, and generate human language.

**Neural Network** -- A computer system loosely inspired by the human brain, made of many small connected units (called neurons) that work together to find patterns in data.

---

## O

**Open Source** -- Software whose code is publicly available for anyone to use, study, or modify. Meta's Llama models are open-source LLMs.

**Overfitting** -- When a model memorizes its training data too closely and fails to work well on new, unseen data. It is like studying only past test answers instead of learning the subject.

---

## P

**Parameter** -- A number inside a model that gets adjusted during training. Large models have billions of parameters -- more parameters generally means the model can learn more complex patterns.

**Prompt** -- The text you type into an AI to get a response. A good prompt tells the AI clearly what you want.

**Prompt Engineering** -- The skill of writing better prompts to get better results from AI. Small changes in how you ask can make a big difference in what you get back.

---

## R

**RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation)** -- A technique where the AI first searches a knowledge base for relevant information, then uses that information to write its answer. This helps reduce hallucinations.

**Reinforcement Learning** -- A training method where an AI learns by trial and error, receiving rewards for good actions and penalties for bad ones, similar to how you might train a pet.

---

## S

**System Prompt** -- Hidden instructions given to an AI before your conversation starts. They set the AI's role, tone, and rules. For example, a system prompt might say "You are a friendly math tutor."

---

## T

**Temperature** -- A setting that controls how creative or random an AI's responses are. Low temperature means more predictable answers; high temperature means more varied and surprising ones.

**Token** -- A small piece of text that an AI processes. A token can be a whole word, part of a word, or even a single character. Most LLMs think in tokens, not words.

**Training Data** -- The massive collection of text, images, or other information used to teach an AI model. The quality and variety of training data directly affects how good the model is.

**Transformer** -- The neural network architecture behind most modern LLMs. It was introduced in 2017 and is especially good at understanding relationships between words in a sentence.

---

## V

**Vector Database** -- A database designed to store and search embeddings (those number-lists that represent meaning). It lets an AI quickly find the most relevant information from a large collection.

---

## W

**Weights** -- Another word for the parameters inside a neural network. During training, the model adjusts its weights to get better at its task.

---

> **Tip:** AI moves fast. New terms appear all the time. When you come across a word you do not know, ask an AI to explain it -- that is one of the things they are best at!
