# Study Plans & Exam Prep

## What You'll Learn

- How to create a realistic, personalized study schedule with AI
- How to break big subjects down into manageable chunks
- How to generate practice questions that actually prepare you for exams
- How to use AI for smarter time management (not just more study time)
- How to recover when you're behind

---

## Why Most Study Plans Fail

You've probably made a study plan before. Wrote it out, maybe even color-coded it. Then life happened, you fell behind on day 2, and you abandoned it by day 4.

The problem isn't discipline — it's that generic plans don't account for your actual life, your strengths and weaknesses, or how long things actually take.

AI can help you build plans that are:
- Realistic (based on the time you actually have)
- Personalized (focused on what you specifically don't know)
- Flexible (easy to adjust when things change)
- Broken down into small enough pieces that you'll actually start

---

## Step 1: Tell AI What You're Working With

The more context you give, the more useful the plan. Start with a setup prompt:

> Prompt: "I have a [SUBJECT] exam in [X days/weeks]. The exam covers: [list topics]. I'm strongest at [TOPIC A] and weakest at [TOPIC B and C]. I can study for about [X hours] per day on [days of the week]. Create a day-by-day study plan that prioritizes my weak areas but still reviews everything."

### Real example:
> Prompt: "I have a history exam in 10 days. It covers: the French Revolution, the Industrial Revolution, and World War 1. I understand WWI okay but I'm really lost on the French Revolution. I can study 1.5 hours on weekdays and 3 hours on weekends. Create a day-by-day study plan."

You'll get a structured schedule you can actually follow.

---

## Step 2: Breaking Subjects Down

Big subjects feel overwhelming because they're one giant blob. AI is great at slicing them into pieces.

> Prompt: "Break down [SUBJECT] into its core topics and subtopics. Organize them from most fundamental (things I need to understand first) to most advanced."

This gives you a learning roadmap — you can see exactly what order to tackle things in.

### For an exam specifically:
> Prompt: "My exam on [SUBJECT] covers [X weeks/chapters] of material. What are the 10 most important concepts I absolutely must understand? Then list the 5 things that are probably on the exam but less critical."

Now you know where to spend your time.

## Study Session Planning (Not Just Day Planning)

It's not enough to block out "study chemistry for 2 hours." You need to know what to do during those 2 hours.

> Prompt: "I have 90 minutes to study [TOPIC] tonight. Design a study session for me. Include: what to review, how long to spend on each part, when to take breaks, and what to do in the last 10 minutes."

A well-designed 90-minute session beats 3 hours of unfocused re-reading every time.

### The Pomodoro approach with AI:
> Prompt: "Design a Pomodoro-style study session for [TOPIC]. That means 25 minutes of focused work followed by a 5-minute break. I have 2 hours total. Tell me exactly what to do in each 25-minute block."

## Breaking Down Subjects: Examples

### For a math-heavy subject:
> Prompt: "I'm studying for a math exam on quadratic equations and functions. Break this into the skills I need to master, in the order I should learn them. For each skill, tell me what I should be able to do when I've mastered it."

### For an essay-based subject:
> Prompt: "I have an English lit exam where I need to write essays about [BOOK/THEME]. Help me break down what I need to prepare: themes to understand, quotes to memorize, essay structures to practice. Turn it into a checklist."

### For a science subject:
> Prompt: "I'm studying biology for a final exam. There are 8 units. Tell me which concepts are most commonly tested and how each unit connects to the others. Help me see the big picture."

## AI-Generated Practice Questions

This is one of the most powerful study tools available. AI can generate unlimited practice questions on demand.

### Basic practice questions:
> Prompt: "Generate 10 practice questions on [TOPIC]. Mix easy, medium, and hard. Include the answers at the end."

### Exam-style questions:
> Prompt: "Write practice questions for [TOPIC] in the exact style of [type of exam — SAT, A-level, IB, etc.]. I need 5 multiple choice and 2 short essay questions."

### Targeting weaknesses:
> Prompt: "I keep getting confused about [SPECIFIC CONCEPT]. Write 5 questions that specifically test this concept from different angles. Include explanations with the answers."

### Surprise questions (to test real understanding):
> Prompt: "Ask me 3 questions about [TOPIC] that would require me to actually understand it deeply, not just memorize facts. The kind of questions that would trip up someone who just crammed."

## Time Management with AI

### Figuring out how long things actually take:
> Prompt: "I need to write a 1000-word history essay, review 3 chapters for a biology test, and do a math problem set. I have 4 hours. How should I divide my time? Give me a realistic breakdown."

### The "I'm behind" rescue plan:
> Prompt: "I have a [SUBJECT] exam in [X days] and I've barely studied. I know almost nothing about [TOPIC A] and [TOPIC B]. What's the minimum I absolutely need to know to pass? Give me an emergency study plan."

### Managing multiple exams:
> Prompt: "I have 3 exams next week: [Subject 1] on Tuesday, [Subject 2] on Thursday, [Subject 3] on Friday. I have about 3 hours of study time each day from now until then. Create a schedule that covers all three."

## Smart Study Strategies (AI Can Teach You These Too)

You can literally ask AI to explain study science to you:

> Prompt: "What are the most proven study techniques according to research? Explain each one briefly and tell me when to use it."

The big ones AI will tell you about (and help you implement):

**Spaced repetition** — reviewing material at increasing intervals
> Prompt: "I have 10 days before my exam. Create a spaced repetition review schedule where I revisit key topics at increasing intervals."

**Active recall** — testing yourself instead of re-reading
> Prompt: "Instead of giving me a summary, ask me questions about [TOPIC] so I have to recall the information myself."

**The Feynman Technique** — explaining concepts simply to test understanding
> Prompt: "I'm going to explain [CONCEPT] to you like you're a 10-year-old. Correct me where I'm wrong and fill in what I miss."

**Interleaving** — mixing up subjects instead of blocking them
> Prompt: "I need to study biology, chemistry, and physics. Instead of doing each one separately, create a mixed study session that switches between them to improve retention."

## Tracking Progress with AI

Use AI as a check-in partner:

> Prompt: "I've been following my study plan for 3 days. Here's what I've covered: [list]. Here's what I'm behind on: [list]. I have [X days] left. Adjust my plan based on this."

> Prompt: "Quiz me on everything I said I was going to study this week. Start easy and get harder. Tell me at the end which areas I'm solid on and which need more work."

---

## The Night Before the Exam

Don't cram. Instead:

> Prompt: "My [SUBJECT] exam is tomorrow. Here are all the topics it covers: [list]. Give me a 1-page cheat sheet — the most important things to remember, organized by topic. Keep it concise."

> Prompt: "Give me 5 questions that are most likely to appear on a [SUBJECT] exam based on the topics I've described. Walk me through the ideal answer structure for each."

> Prompt: "What are the most common mistakes students make on [SUBJECT] exams? Tell me what to watch out for tomorrow."

## Exercises

- [ ] Take your next exam or major assignment deadline. Use the setup prompt to build a full day-by-day study plan. Actually follow it for a week and note how it goes.

- [ ] Pick one subject you find difficult. Ask AI to break it down into core topics and subtopics. Turn it into a checklist and check off topics as you cover them.

- [ ] Generate 10 practice questions on a topic you're studying. Answer them without looking anything up. Then check your answers and ask AI to explain the ones you got wrong.

- [ ] Try the Feynman Technique with AI: pick a concept, explain it out loud (or typed) in the simplest way you can, and ask AI to correct you and fill in gaps.

- [ ] If you're behind on something right now: use the "emergency study plan" prompt. Give AI your real situation — how many days, how much you know, how much time you have. Follow the plan.

## Homework

1. Build a study plan for your next upcoming exam using everything from this lesson
2. Do at least 3 AI-generated practice question sessions before the exam

4. The night before the exam: generate a 1-page cheat sheet with AI
5. After the exam: reflect on what worked and what you'd change about the study process

**The goal isn't to study more — it's to study smarter. AI makes that actually possible.**
