## From Experiments to Real Outputs

In the last lesson, you learned how prompts work. Now we put that into practice. By the end of this lesson, you'll have created actual usable graphics — not just cool AI experiments.

The workflow we'll use every time:
1. **Define the goal** — what does this graphic need to do?
2. **Generate with AI** — create the raw visual
3. **Refine with Canva** — add text, adjust, finalize
4. **Export and use** — real outputs, not just screenshots

Let's go through each project type.

## Project 1: YouTube Thumbnails

Thumbnails are the single most impactful thing you can optimize for YouTube growth. A bad thumbnail on a great video gets ignored. A great thumbnail on a decent video gets clicked.

### What makes a great thumbnail:
- **High contrast** — dark and light clash, or bright on simple background
- **One clear focal point** — viewer knows immediately where to look
- **Expressive face** — if there's a person, they have a strong emotion
- **Bold, readable colors** — visible even at small size
- **Minimal text** — 3-5 words max on the thumbnail itself (not in your AI generation, just the final Canva edit)

### Step 1: Generate the base image
> Prompt: "YouTube thumbnail background for a video about [topic]. High contrast, dramatic lighting. Bold, vibrant colors. [Describe the main visual element — person, object, scene]. No text in the image. Clean area on one side for text overlay. Style: modern YouTube thumbnail aesthetic, ultra sharp, 16:9 ratio."

> Prompt: "YouTube thumbnail featuring a person with a shocked/excited expression, pointing to the left side of the frame (where I'll add text). Bright studio lighting. Bold color background — electric blue or fire orange. Photorealistic. No text. 16:9."

### Step 2: Refine in Canva
1. Open Canva — set up a YouTube Thumbnail template (1280x720px)
2. Upload your AI-generated image as the background
3. Add bold title text (font: thick, readable, high contrast — try "Anton" or "Bebas Neue")
4. Add a colored box behind the text if the background is busy
5. Export as JPG

### Design tip: The thumbnail equation
```
Strong visual + 3-word title + high contrast = more clicks
```

## Project 2: Presentation Backgrounds

Boring presentations lose your audience in the first 30 seconds. AI-generated backgrounds can transform a Google Slides or PowerPoint deck instantly.

### Types of presentation backgrounds:

**Clean gradient background:**
> Prompt: "Abstract background for a presentation slide. Soft gradient from deep navy blue to purple. Minimal, modern, professional. No text, no clutter. 16:9 ratio. High resolution."

**Themed background for a specific topic:**
> Prompt: "Presentation background for a slide about climate change. Visual: abstract representation of earth, ocean, and sky blending together. Muted earth tones, slightly dramatic. Room for text overlay in center. 16:9 ratio."

**Corporate / professional style:**
> Prompt: "Clean professional presentation background. Light gray with subtle geometric shapes. Minimalist business aesthetic. Soft shadows. Perfect for a white text overlay. 16:9."

**Bold and creative:**
> Prompt: "Dynamic abstract background for a creative presentation. Explosive burst of colors: electric blue, neon yellow, hot pink. Energy and movement. Urban, modern, Gen Z aesthetic. 16:9."

### Step 3: Building the full deck in Google Slides
1. Generate 3-4 different backgrounds (intro, content, conclusion, call-to-action)
2. Import into Google Slides as image fills for each slide
3. Create a consistent text style (one font for headers, one for body)
4. Result: a presentation that looks custom-designed

## Project 3: Social Media Post Graphics

Three types of social media graphics and how to generate each:

### Quote cards (Instagram / Twitter)

> Prompt: "Instagram quote card background. Aesthetic gradient: warm terracotta to blush pink. Soft, feminine, minimal. Clean center area for text overlay. No text in image. Square format (1:1 ratio). High resolution."

> Prompt: "Instagram quote card background. Dark, moody aesthetic. Deep charcoal to black gradient. Subtle texture, like brushed concrete. Masculine, minimal. Clean space for bold white text. 1:1 ratio."

### Carousel backgrounds (Instagram)

Instagram carousels look best when slides feel like a visual series — same palette, slightly different layouts.

> Prompt: "Set of 5 consistent Instagram carousel slide backgrounds. Theme: minimal and clean, earthy tones (cream, beige, terracotta). Each slide should look slightly different but cohesive as a series. No text. 1:1 ratio."

(Generate them one at a time or ask for variations of a starting background)

### Story backgrounds (Instagram / TikTok)

> Prompt: "Instagram story background. Abstract flowing shapes, gradient from electric blue to deep purple. Dynamic, modern. 9:16 ratio (vertical). No text, large clean area in the center."

> Prompt: "Instagram story template background. Bright, summery. Coral, yellow, and white. Fun, youthful, beach vibes. 9:16 ratio."

## Project 4: Simple Logo Concepts

AI is great for generating logo *concepts* and *icon ideas*. It's not reliable for generating the final text portion of logos (it still struggles with accurate letter forms in images). So the workflow is: generate the icon/symbol, add your wordmark in Canva.

### Icon/symbol concepts:

> Prompt: "Minimalist logo icon for a brand called 'Flux' — a streetwear and lifestyle brand for young men. The icon should be abstract, bold, modern. Think geometric shapes, not literal objects. Black and white only. Clean vector-style. No text in the image."

> Prompt: "Simple logo icon for a personal fitness brand. Concept: a rising sun or upward arrow, representing growth and energy. Minimal, strong, versatile. Should work at small sizes. Black on white background."

> Prompt: "Logo icon for a student productivity app. Concept: an open book combined with a lightning bolt, representing learning and speed. Flat design style, 2 colors max. Modern and clean."

### Step 4: Finishing in Canva
1. Upload your AI icon to Canva
2. Create a new design (custom size: 500x500px for a square logo)
3. Place the icon on the left or top
4. Add your brand name in a strong font (Canva has hundreds of free fonts)
5. Adjust colors to match your brand palette
6. Download as PNG with transparent background

## The Canva + AI Workflow (Full Breakdown)

Canva is the tool that bridges your AI-generated images and professional-looking finished graphics. Here's how to use them together:

### Canva features you need to know:

**Background Remover** (Canva Pro)
- Upload your AI-generated image
- Click "Edit Image" → "Background Remover"
- Removes the background so you can place your subject anywhere
- Great for combining AI-generated people/objects with your own backgrounds

**Magic Media (Canva's own AI)**
- Inside Canva: Apps → Magic Media
- Generate images without leaving Canva
- Good for quick backgrounds and textures

**Text Effects**
- Canva has built-in text animations and effects
- Shadow, glow, lift, echo — all make text more readable on busy backgrounds

**Brand Kit** (Canva Pro)
- Store your brand colors, fonts, and logo in one place
- Apply your brand to any design in one click
- Keeps everything consistent

**Grids and Frames**
- Drag your AI images into grid frames for professional photo collages
- Great for Instagram carousels and mood boards

This is the full exercise — creating a complete branded graphics package from scratch.

**Define your brand:** Choose one of these fictional brands to work with (or invent your own):
- A study/productivity page called "SharpMind"

2. **Instagram post background** — on-brand aesthetic, 1:1
3. **Instagram story background** — on-brand, 9:16
4. **YouTube thumbnail** — if it's a content creator brand, 16:9

**The process for each:**
1. Write a brand brief first: colors, tone, audience, aesthetic words
2. Generate AI images that match the brief
3. Bring them into Canva to finalize
4. Make sure all 5 pieces feel like they belong to the same brand

## Design Principles for Non-Designers

You don't need design school. You need to know 5 rules:

**1. Contrast** — Light vs. dark. Bright vs. muted. Big vs. small. If two things look similar, one of them is wrong.

**2. Hierarchy** — Make the most important thing the biggest. Second most important: medium. Everything else: small.

**3. Whitespace** — Empty space is not wasted space. It makes everything else breathe. Cramped = bad. Spacious = professional.

**4. Consistency** — Pick 2 fonts and stick to them. Pick 3 colors and stick to them. If in doubt, repeat what already works.

**5. Alignment** — Things should line up. Left edges should match. Centers should center. Misaligned text looks amateur instantly.

> Prompt: "Give me feedback on this graphic I'm designing: [describe it in detail]. Based on design principles like contrast, hierarchy, whitespace, and alignment, what could I improve?"

You can literally use AI to critique your own designs.
