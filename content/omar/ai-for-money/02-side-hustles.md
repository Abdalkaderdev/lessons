## What Is a Side Hustle?

A side hustle is a way to earn money outside of a traditional job.
It could be a service, a product, or a skill you sell to people who need it.

At 17, side hustles are particularly powerful because:
- You have no mortgage, no dependents, no big bills
- You can afford to try things and fail fast
- Skills you build now compound over years
- Every pound you earn goes directly to you

People earn £50 to £5,000+ per month from side hustles. The difference is not luck.
It is picking the right skill, building a portfolio, and consistently finding clients.

## The Best Freelancing Skills for Beginners

These skills are in high demand and learnable quickly with AI assistance:

### 1. AI-Assisted Web Design

What you sell: Simple websites for small businesses (restaurants, barbers, tutors, local shops)

Tools you already know: Claude, v0.dev, Bolt.new, Vercel

What clients pay: £100–£500 per site for a basic 3–5 page website

Why beginners can do it: You built 3 working websites in the coding module.
Most local small businesses do not have a website. You can build one in 2–3 hours with AI.

> Prompt: "A local [type of business, e.g. barber shop] near me has no website. Write me a sales pitch I could use to approach them in person or via email. Keep it short — 3 sentences. Tell them what I'll build, how long it takes, and what it costs. Make it sound confident, not like a teenager asking for a favour."

### 2. Social Media Content Creation

What you sell: Writing captions, creating graphics, scheduling posts for small businesses

Tools: Claude (captions and copy), Canva ([canva.com](https://canva.com)) — free plan, DALL-E or Midjourney for images

What clients pay: £100–£400/month for a basic social media package

> Prompt: "I want to offer social media management to small local businesses. Write me a simple service package I can offer: three tiers (basic, standard, premium) with what's included at each level and what to charge. I would create posts using Canva and AI-generated captions. The client approves before I post. Keep it simple — I am 17 with no agency experience but I can deliver good work."

### 3. Copywriting and Content Writing

What you sell: Blog posts, product descriptions, email newsletters, website copy

Tools: Claude, [jasper.ai](https://jasper.ai) (free trial), [copy.ai](https://copy.ai) (free plan)

What clients pay: £20–£80 per article, £0.05–£0.20 per word

> Prompt: "I want to offer content writing services as a freelancer. I'll use AI tools to help write and then edit everything myself for quality and accuracy. Write me 3 sample short blog post introductions (100 words each) on these topics: (1) why local businesses need a website, (2) how small shops can use Instagram, (3) tips for eating healthy on a student budget. I'll use these as portfolio samples."

### 4. Video Editing

What you sell: Editing short-form videos for creators, businesses, and schools

Tools: CapCut ([capcut.com](https://capcut.com)) — free, powerful, AI features built in

What clients pay: £30–£150 per video depending on length and complexity

> Prompt: "I want to offer video editing as a side hustle. I use CapCut and can edit videos up to 10 minutes. I'm 17 with no professional clients yet. Write me a list of 10 types of clients who need video editing, where to find them, and what to say to get my first client. Focus on realistic first steps, not a big agency pitch."

### 5. Tutoring

What you sell: Help with subjects you are good at for younger students

What clients pay: £10–£25/hour in the UK

Who needs it: GCSE students, primary school children, students struggling with maths or English

> Prompt: "I'm 17 and good at [subject]. I want to start tutoring younger students. Help me write: (1) a short bio I can share with parents, (2) a simple tutoring session structure I can follow, (3) a list of places where I can advertise my tutoring for free locally and online."

## Step 3: Set Up on Freelance Platforms

### Fiverr

**URL:** [fiverr.com](https://fiverr.com)

Fiverr lets you list services ("gigs") that clients browse and buy.
You set the price. Fiverr takes 20%.

**Age requirement:** 13+ with parental consent. 17 is fine.

How to set up your first gig:

> Prompt: "Write a Fiverr gig listing for a web design service. Gig title: 'I will build you a modern, mobile-friendly website using AI tools'. Gig description: explains what I'll deliver, that I use AI tools to build fast and professionally, what the process is (brief, build, review, deliver). Packages: Basic (1-page landing page, £75), Standard (3-page website, £150), Premium (5-page website with contact form, £250). Frequently asked questions section with 3 realistic questions and answers. Make it professional and specific — not vague."

### Upwork

**URL:** [upwork.com](https://upwork.com)

Upwork is better for longer projects and higher rates. More competitive than Fiverr.
You apply to specific jobs rather than waiting for clients to find you.

**Age requirement:** 18. You can set this up now, ready for when you turn 18.

> Prompt: "Write an Upwork profile overview for a freelance web designer and content writer. I'm a student who builds websites and writes content for small businesses using AI tools. I've built 5+ web projects. My rates are £15–25/hour for content writing, £150–500 per project for websites. Keep it 150 words. Professional, confident, specific about skills and tools used."

### Freelancer.com

**URL:** [freelancer.com](https://freelancer.com)

Similar to Upwork. Good for finding your first few clients.

**Age requirement:** 16+

## Step 4: Your First Client

Freelance platforms are competitive. Your first client will likely come from
your real-life network.

### The 20-Business Outreach Method

Write a list of 20 local businesses you visit or know about.
Check if they have a website. If not, or if their site is outdated — that is a lead.

> Prompt: "Write a short, friendly outreach message I can send on Instagram DM to a local business that has no website. I'm 17 and offering to build them a simple 3-page website for £150. The message should: be casual and direct (not a copy-paste sales pitch), mention that I can show them examples of my work, and offer a free 15-minute call to discuss. Max 5 sentences."

Send this to 20 businesses. If 2 reply and 1 becomes a client, you have made £150 from a cold outreach.
That is a legitimate business skill used by professional sales people worldwide.

## Step 5: Create a Simple Business Plan

A business plan does not need to be 20 pages long. For a side hustle, one page is enough.

> Prompt: "Help me write a one-page business plan for my freelance web design side hustle. Include: (1) What I do (service description), (2) Who I serve (target clients), (3) How I'll find clients (channels), (4) What I charge (pricing), (5) Monthly income target and how many clients I need to hit it, (6) Tools I'll use to deliver the work, (7) 90-day plan with specific milestones. Keep each section to 2–3 sentences. Make it practical — no corporate language."

Print this and put it somewhere visible. Review it monthly.

## Managing Money From a Side Hustle

Once you start earning, track it properly from the start.

> Prompt: "I'm starting a small freelance business at 17 in the UK. Help me understand: (1) when do I need to register as self-employed or pay tax? (2) How should I track my income and expenses? (3) What is the personal allowance in the UK and how does it affect me? (4) Should I use a separate bank account for business money? Give me practical, accurate UK advice."

Key facts for the UK:
- Personal allowance: £12,570/year before you pay any income tax (2024/25)
- Under this threshold: you do not pay income tax, but you should still register if you earn regularly
- Keep records of every payment in and every expense out
- Use a free tool like [Wave](https://waveapps.com) or a Google Sheets invoice template
