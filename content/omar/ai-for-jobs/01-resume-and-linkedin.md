# Resume, CV & LinkedIn with AI

Most 17-year-olds think they have nothing to put on a CV. They are wrong.

You have skills, projects, interests, and experiences. AI helps you turn them into a
document that sounds professional, is tailored to the job, and actually gets read.

## CV vs Resume — What's the Difference?

**CV (Curriculum Vitae):** A full record of everything — education, skills, projects,
activities, awards. Used in the UK, Europe, and for academic or professional applications.
Usually 1–2 pages for someone your age.

**Resume:** A shorter, targeted document tailored to one specific job.
Typically 1 page. Used mainly in the US and for tech/startup roles.

At 17, you will mostly use a **CV** for jobs, apprenticeships, and university applications.
When applying to US companies or tech startups, use a **resume**.

Today you will build both using AI — the CV first, then show AI how to shorten it into a resume.

## What Goes on a 17-Year-Old's CV?

You do not need work experience. Here is what you have that counts:

**Education**
- School name, years attended, predicted or actual grades
- Relevant subjects (Maths, Computing, Business, Art, etc.)

**Projects**
- Calculator app, quiz app, portfolio page (you built these in the coding module)
- School projects, art projects, anything you made

**Skills**
- AI tools (Claude, v0.dev, Bolt.new)
- Computer skills (Word, Excel, Google Docs)
- Languages you speak
- Creative skills (photography, video editing, design)

**Activities & Interests**
- Sports teams, clubs, volunteering
- YouTube channel, Instagram page, creative projects
- Part-time help at family business, babysitting, tutoring younger siblings

**Achievements**
- Awards, certificates, competitions
- School leadership roles (prefect, class representative, team captain)

**References**
- A teacher, youth worker, coach, or family friend who can confirm your character

## Step 1: Brain Dump Everything

Before using AI, make a list. Do not filter — write everything.

Open Claude at [claude.ai](https://claude.ai) and paste this:

> Prompt: "I'm 17 and creating my first CV. I have no official work experience. Help me think of everything worth putting on a CV at my age. Ask me questions one at a time about my education, skills, projects, hobbies, and achievements so I can give you the raw material to build my CV from."

Answer Claude's questions honestly. Once you have given all the information, say:

> Prompt: "Now take everything I've told you and write a professional CV for me. Use a clean, modern format. Sections: Personal Statement (3–4 sentences), Education, Skills, Projects, Activities & Interests, Achievements, References (available on request). Write in UK English. Make it sound professional but not fake — it should still sound like a motivated 17-year-old, not a 40-year-old executive."

## Step 2: Write a Personal Statement

The personal statement is the first thing employers read. It is 3–4 sentences that answer:
- Who are you?
- What are you good at / interested in?
- What are you looking for?

Most people write boring statements. Here is how to write a good one with AI.

> Prompt: "Write a personal statement for my CV. I am 17, studying at [your school]. I am interested in technology, AI tools, and creative projects. I am looking for a part-time job, work experience, or apprenticeship in tech, retail, or a creative field. I am reliable, quick to learn, and already use professional AI tools to build web projects. Keep it to 4 sentences. Do not use clichés like 'hardworking team player' — make it specific and genuine."

---

## Step 3: Format It Professionally

A CV with messy formatting gets ignored even if the content is great.

Ask Claude to give you a formatted version:

> Prompt: "Format my CV professionally. Use clear section headings in bold. Use bullet points for skills and achievements. Use a consistent date format (Month Year). Keep it to one page. Give me the final CV as plain text that I can copy into a Word document or Google Doc."

Then paste it into **Google Docs** ([docs.google.com](https://docs.google.com)):
1. Open a new document
2. Paste the text
3. Apply a clean font (Calibri or Georgia, size 11)
4. Add your name in large bold text at the top
5. Add your email and phone number under your name
6. Download as PDF: File → Download → PDF

---

## Step 4: Tailor for Different Jobs

The same CV sent to every job is weaker than a tailored CV.

When you find a job you want, copy the job description and use this prompt:

> Prompt: "Here is my CV: [paste CV]. Here is the job description I'm applying for: [paste job description]. Rewrite my CV so it is tailored to this specific job. Highlight the skills and experiences that are most relevant. Adjust the personal statement to mention the company name and why I want this specific role. Keep everything truthful — do not add things I haven't done."

This takes 2 minutes and dramatically increases your chances of getting an interview.

## Step 5: LinkedIn Profile

LinkedIn is the professional version of Instagram. Recruiters use it to find candidates.
Even at 17, having a profile makes you look serious and professional.

**URL:** [linkedin.com](https://linkedin.com) — free to join

### Set up your profile

1. Sign up at [linkedin.com](https://linkedin.com)
2. Use a real photo (professional — not a selfie at a party)
3. Add your school under "Education"
4. Add your skills (AI tools, languages, software)
5. Add your projects (with links to your GitHub or Vercel URLs)

### Write your LinkedIn headline

The headline is the line under your name. Most people put their job title.
You do not have one yet, so use what you are building:

> Prompt: "Write a LinkedIn headline for a 17-year-old student interested in AI, web development, and creative tech. I build web apps using AI tools and want to work in tech. Keep it under 15 words. Do not use 'aspiring' — that sounds weak. Make it confident and specific."

Good examples:
- "17 | Building web apps with AI | Open to tech internships"
- "Student developer | AI tools | v0.dev, Claude, Vercel"
- "AI-powered builder | Websites & apps | Seeking work experience"

### Write your LinkedIn About section

> Prompt: "Write a LinkedIn 'About' section for my profile. I'm 17, studying at [school]. I build web projects using AI tools like Claude, v0.dev, and Bolt.new. I've built a calculator app, a quiz app, and a personal portfolio page. I'm looking for part-time work, internships, or apprenticeships in tech or creative fields. Write 3 short paragraphs. First paragraph: who I am and what I build. Second: my skills and tools. Third: what I'm looking for. Keep it confident, honest, and not generic."

### Add your projects to LinkedIn

Under "Projects":
- Add your portfolio page (paste the Vercel URL)
- Add the quiz app
- Add the calculator app

A recruiter clicking on a working link is 10x more powerful than just listing "web development" as a skill.

## Step 6: Writing a Compelling Bio (For Anywhere)

A bio is a short paragraph about you — used on LinkedIn, Instagram, your portfolio page,
your GitHub profile, and anywhere else you introduce yourself online.

> Prompt: "Write three versions of a short bio for me: (1) A professional 2-sentence bio for LinkedIn and my portfolio (50 words max). (2) A casual Instagram-style bio (3 lines, punchy, can use emojis). (3) A GitHub profile bio (technical-sounding, 2 sentences, mentions AI tools and projects). My details: name is Omar, age 17, interested in AI, coding, and creative tech, based in the UK."

Use the version that matches where you are posting it.

## Cover Letters

When applying for jobs online, many applications ask for a cover letter — a short email or
letter explaining why you want the job.

> Prompt: "Write a cover letter for me. I'm 17 applying for a part-time [job title] at [company name]. The job involves [describe the role]. I have no official work experience but I am reliable, eager to learn, and already use professional-level AI tools to build web projects. Keep it to 3 short paragraphs. Professional but not stuffy. Do not start with 'I am writing to express my interest' — that is too old-fashioned."

Customise the details. Takes 3 minutes. Far better than writing from scratch.
