# 🧠 Quiz Yourself with AI

### Studying for a test? Make it a game instead!

---

## What You'll Learn

- How to ask AI to create quizzes on any topic
- How to make multiple choice, true/false, and fill-in-the-blank questions
- How to make your quiz harder or easier
- Why quizzing yourself is one of the best ways to study

---

## Why Quizzes Are Better Than Just Re-Reading

Here's a weird fact: re-reading your notes is actually one of the WORST ways to study.

Your brain gets lazy. It sees the words and thinks "yep, I know this." But then on the test — blank.

**Quizzing yourself is different.** When you try to answer a question, your brain has to actually search for the answer. That searching is what makes memories stick.

Scientists call it the "testing effect." We call it: **studying that actually works.**

And now you can get AI to make unlimited quizzes on any topic, anytime. Let's go!

---

## How to Ask AI for a Quiz

It's really simple. Just tell AI:

> Prompt: "Make me a 5-question quiz about the solar system. I'm in 5th grade."

> Prompt: "Quiz me on multiplication tables, 6 through 9. Give me 10 questions one at a time."

> Prompt: "I have a test on the American Revolution next week. Give me a quiz with 6 questions."

AI will ask the questions. You answer. Then AI tells you if you got it right and explains the correct answer if you were wrong.

---

## Types of Quizzes You Can Make

### 1. Multiple Choice

This is the classic format. AI gives you a question and 4 options — you pick the right one.

> Prompt: "Give me a 5-question multiple choice quiz about animal habitats. Give me one question at a time and wait for my answer."

**Why it helps:** Even if you're not sure, you might recognize the right answer — and that helps you learn.

---

### 2. True or False

Fast and fun. AI makes a statement — you say if it's true or false.

> Prompt: "Give me 10 true or false questions about World War 2. Go one at a time."

**Why it helps:** Great for learning facts quickly. And you've got a 50/50 chance of being right!

---

### 3. Fill in the Blank

AI gives you a sentence with a missing word — you fill it in.

> Prompt: "Give me fill-in-the-blank questions about the human body. Like: 'The organ that pumps blood is called the ___.' Make 6 questions."

**Why it helps:** You have to actually recall the word — not just recognize it. Harder, but way better for your memory.

---

### 4. Rapid Fire

No multiple choice — just straight questions, fast!

> Prompt: "Rapid fire quiz: ask me 10 questions about the planets. Go fast, one at a time, just ask — don't give me options."

**Why it helps:** Trains your brain to pull out facts quickly — perfect for timed tests!

---

## Making Your Quiz Harder or Easier

### Too easy? Level up!

> Prompt: "That was too easy. Make the next quiz harder — add trick questions and less obvious choices."

> Prompt: "Give me harder questions about fractions. Include questions about mixed numbers."

### Too hard? Dial it back!

> Prompt: "This is too hard for me right now. Can you make easier questions on the same topic and explain the answers as we go?"

> Prompt: "I only know the basics of fractions. Start with really simple questions and get slowly harder."

You're in charge of your own difficulty level. How cool is that?

---

## The "Boss Level" Quiz

Once you feel confident on a topic, try this challenge:

> Prompt: "Give me a really tough quiz on [topic] — like a final exam level. 10 questions. No hints. Go!"

If you pass, you're READY for the real test.

---

## Quiz-Then-Explain: The Power Move

Here's an upgrade: after you answer a question, whether right or wrong, ask AI to explain WHY.

> Prompt: "Give me a quiz on the water cycle. After each answer I give, explain why the right answer is correct."

This turns every question into a mini lesson. You don't just find out you were wrong — you actually understand WHY.

---

## Study Session Idea: The 15-Minute Quiz Challenge

Got a test coming up? Try this:

1. Open a chat with Claude
2. Tell it what your test is about and when it is
3. Say: "Let's do a 15-minute study session. Make me a quiz and keep going until I've answered 20 questions."
4. Track your score as you go
5. At the end: "Which topics did I get wrong? Can you focus on those next time?"

That last step is powerful — AI can remember what you struggled with in the conversation and focus on your weak spots!

## Exercises

- [ ] Pick a topic you have a test on soon (or something you studied this week). Ask AI to give you a 5-question multiple choice quiz.
- [ ] Try at least 2 different quiz formats: multiple choice, true/false, fill-in-the-blank, or rapid fire.
- [ ] After the quiz, ask AI: "What topics should I study more based on my answers?"
- [ ] Challenge yourself: ask for a harder quiz on the same topic and see if you do better the second time.

---

## Homework

Before your next test or exam, do a full AI study session:

1. Tell AI the subject and what you've learned so far.
2. Ask for a 10-question quiz on the topic.
3. Answer every question — don't skip!
4. Ask AI to summarize what you got right and where you need more practice.
5. Do one more focused quiz on only the topics you missed.

Then go take that test with confidence. You've got this! 💪
